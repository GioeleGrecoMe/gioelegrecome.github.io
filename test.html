Abstract

[cite_start]Accurate and efficient spatial acoustic characterization is crucial for immersive audio applications, such as virtual and augmented reality, which require real-time, high-fidelity rendering of sound fields[cite: 12]. [cite_start]Traditional methods, including physics-based simulations and centralized deep learning models, face significant challenges in scalability, data efficiency, and generalization under sparse measurement conditions[cite: 13]. [cite_start]This paper introduces FAME, a Federated Acoustic Mixture-of-Experts framework designed for large-area spatial acoustic modeling[cite: 14]. [cite_start]FAME decomposes the global sound field reconstruction task into localized sub-problems, each handled by a specialized neural network expert trained on region-specific data[cite: 15]. [cite_start]A lightweight gating network intelligently combines these experts' predictions based on spatial queries, enabling seamless integration of new measurements without full retraining[cite: 16]. [cite_start]Evaluated on a benchmark dataset of higher-order ambisonic room impulse responses, FAME significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in reconstruction accuracy, directional error, and energy decay preservation[cite: 17]. [cite_start]The framework offers a scalable, data-efficient solution for real-time spatial audio applications, supporting incremental expansion and robust performance in sparse-data regimes[cite: 18].

Method Overview

[cite_start]The key innovation of FAME is the decomposition of the complex spatial prediction task into localized sub-problems handled by specialized "expert" networks[cite: 31, 36]. [cite_start]The measurement space is partitioned into distinct receiver regions, and each expert is trained only on the Higher Order Ambisonic (HOA) RIRs from its assigned region[cite: 36, 55]. [cite_start]A lightweight, learnable gating network then intelligently blends the contributions of these experts to form a globally consistent prediction[cite: 37, 43]. [cite_start]This modular architecture avoids the need to retrain the entire model when expanding to new areas; one only needs to train a new local expert and update the gating mechanism[cite: 39, 44].

Figure 1: The FAME framework. Data from distinct regions (Agents) are used to train local experts. A gating network learns to weigh their predictions to form the final output.

Results

[cite_start]We evaluated FAME against several state-of-the-art methods in a challenging sparse-data regime, using only 8 HOA measurements for training[cite: 136, 165]. [cite_start]The remaining 17 measurements were used for testing[cite: 137]. [cite_start]The results demonstrate FAME's superior performance in reconstruction accuracy (NMSE), spatial accuracy (DoA Error), and reverberation modeling (EDR Error)[cite: 166]. [cite_start]In contrast to interpolation methods like PVM and PCKI which degrade rapidly when extrapolating, FAME maintains high accuracy by effectively bridging large unmeasured gaps between locally dense data clusters[cite: 171, 238].
