The Bifurcation Diagram

Nonlinear Dynamics in Musical Acoustics

Reading the Map

  • Control Parameter ($P_{mouth}$): Energy input. As we move right, we blow harder.
  • State Variable ($P_{rms}$): The "Loudness". A value of 0 means silence (equilibrium).
  • The Fork (Bifurcation): At a critical pressure, the silent solution becomes unstable. The system jumps to a "Limit Cycle" (the blue branches).
  • Hysteresis: Note the difference between the Blue Dot (Start) and the Red Dot (Stop). Once the reed is vibrating, it's easier to keep it going than to start it.
$$ M\ddot{x} + R\dot{x} + Kx = \Delta P(t) \cdot S_{reed} $$
  • Mass ($M$): Represents the inertia of the reed or lips.
  • Stiffness ($K$): Represents the elasticity (how hard it is to bend).
  • The Rule of Thumb:
    • Inward Striking (Reeds): The playing frequency is below the reed's natural frequency ($\omega < \omega_r$). The motion is controlled by stiffness ($K$).
    • Outward Striking (Lips): The playing frequency is above the lip's natural resonance ($\omega > \omega_r$). The motion is controlled by inertia ($M$).
